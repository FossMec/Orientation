How does open source make a student's life better ?

1. Helpful Community

Open Source community is very helpful, so, students new to programming can take help from a lot of other people to get work done.

2. It’s generally free.

How does being free make a big deal ?

  • Usually offered for free & don’t require you to pay for any additional copy you download unlike windows and mac operating systems.
  • Can lengthen the life of old hardware and avoid the need to replace them.
  • It has been estimated that open source software collectively saves businesses $60 billion a year.

3. Created by skillful and talented people.

  • Developers prefer to work on open source products as they can be an outlet for their ideas and creativity.
  • They won’t be confined by the rigid rules of the corporate world, and they’ll have the freedom to experiment and come up with high-quality programs.
  • Students and other consumers get access to world-class and state-of-the-art software without having to pay too much or pay at all.

4. Highly reliable

5. Provides full freedom

  • You can modify and adapt open source software for your own requirements.
  • You can mix and match your software and create a unique IT infrastructure that best suits your needs.
  • Gives users the freedom to study how the program works by accessing the source code, writing additional code, testing, modifying and distributing it.
  • These things are prohibited for proprietary software.

    • 6. No imposed upgrades

  • Free software never disappears like proprietary.
  • If proprietary vendors stop supporting a product, users have two options: either use an unsupported version of the software, or go for an (unwanted) upgrade. Imposed upgrading never happens for free software.
  • For example when RedHat decided to stop supporting their RedHat Linux 7, 8, and 9, various other companies came forward to support them.
  • Taking Windows for another example, it keeps giving you reminders about constant updates, and after every update you would need to restart your computer.

    • 7. No spying on users

  • The company behind proprietary software often installs features that restrict users from sharing it with others.
  • Anyone who buys proprietary software must sign a licensing agreement before using it, they are agreeing that the vendor has the right to inspect hard drive content without warning.
  • This violates our privacy because our computers hold our personal information and daily activities.

    • 8. Auditability

  • Users are forced to believe, when closed-source software manufacturers claim that they made improvements in the program, improved security and restricted backdoors.
  • Since the source code is not provided, there is no evidence for such claims, whereas the code for Open source operating systems can be checked by us at any point of time.
  • Because the software is secret, all users are dependent on the corporation to fix these kinds of problems.

    • 9. No monopolies

  • It is easier and cheaper to switch from one free software to another compared to switching proprietary software.
  • Free software does not tie you down to any corporation.
